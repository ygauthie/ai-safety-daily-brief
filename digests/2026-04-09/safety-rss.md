# AI Safety Digest - April 9, 2026

## Top Stories

### 1. [Claude Mythos Security Vulnerabilities Raise Major Concerns](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/2wi5mCLSkZo2ky32p/do-not-be-surprised-if-lesswrong-gets-hacked)
Anthropic has announced Claude Mythos, accompanied by a concerning blog post from their Frontier Red Team detailing numerous zero-day exploits and security vulnerabilities discovered by the AI system. A LessWrong administrator is warning the community about potential security breaches, suggesting the cybersecurity landscape may be fundamentally shifting with more capable AI systems able to discover and exploit vulnerabilities at scale.

### 2. [Current State Assessment: "My Picture of the Present in AI"](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/WjaGAA4xCAXeFpyWm/my-picture-of-the-present-in-ai)
An Alignment Forum contributor provides a comprehensive "scenario forecast for the present" - acknowledging that even the current state of AI in April 2026 is uncertain and requires forecasting. This meta-commentary on our epistemic position suggests rapid change and opacity in AI capabilities development.

### 3. [Community Soul-Searching: "Have We Already Lost?"](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/tjZWbmuCE9tjYQsrf/have-we-already-lost-part-1-the-plan-in-2024)
A reflection from the Inkhaven Residency examining whether the AI Safety community has passed a "point of no return" where AGI risk becomes both likely and outside community control. While the author concludes "no" (following Betteridge's Law), the prominence of this question indicates significant concern about the field's trajectory and effectiveness.

### 4. [Public Opinion Research: "101 Humans of New York on AI Risks"](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/BCiDwMbvq5JRNAwwt/101-humans-of-new-york-on-the-risks-of-ai)
The first door-to-door survey on AI risks provides ground-truth data on public perception, offering insights beyond typical online polling. This research addresses a crucial gap in understanding how ordinary citizens view AI risks, which has implications for democratic governance and policy approaches.

### 5. [Unsupervised AI Evaluation Challenges](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/ASoFTyk3bzBE62dyn/my-unsupervised-elicitation-challenge)
A researcher using Claude Opus 4.6 for Ancient Greek tutoring has discovered systematic errors but is testing whether others can identify these mistakes without domain expertise. This highlights the challenge of evaluating AI systems in specialized domains where human supervisors may lack expertise.

## Key Arguments

### **AI Development as "Trojan Horse Race"**
The argument that AI development isn't a traditional "arms race" where speed is inherently beneficial, but rather a "[Trojan horse race](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/z3xbNMk9R2fP3aqYf/ai-as-a-trojan-horse-race)" where rushing may be self-defeating even from competitive perspectives.

### **Regulatory Precedents Exist**
Strong pushback against "we can't prevent progress" narratives, with the [FDA cited as proof](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/aYfTyppBMgyZQAQrv/we-can-prevent-progress-conceptual-clarity-and-inspiration) that technological development can be successfully regulated when safety requires it.

### **Cybersecurity Paradigm Shift**
The Mythos announcement suggests AI systems may fundamentally alter cybersecurity by discovering vulnerabilities at unprecedented scale, potentially requiring new defensive approaches across all digital infrastructure.

## Community Discussion

### **Evaluation and Oversight Challenges**
The unsupervised elicitation challenge exemplifies growing concerns about evaluating AI systems in domains where human overseers lack expertise - a problem that will likely worsen as AI capabilities expand into increasingly specialized areas.

### **Meta-Level Uncertainty**
The need for "scenario forecasting for the present" indicates how rapidly the AI landscape is changing and how difficult it has become to maintain situational awareness even about current capabilities, let alone future developments.

### **Community Morale and Strategy**
The "have we already lost?" framing suggests significant anxiety within the AI safety community about whether current approaches are adequate, though the discourse remains focused on solutions rather than despair.

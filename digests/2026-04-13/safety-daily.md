# Daily Brief (2026-04-13)

## Top 3 Developments

1. **Anthropic's "Mythos" Model Signals End of Public AI Access Era**: According to analysis on LessWrong, [Anthropic's latest model "Mythos" represents state-of-the-art capabilities but will not be publicly available](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/3MhJELzwpbR42xsJ3/the-policy-surrounding-mythos-marks-an-irreversible-power), instead being limited to select companies through "project glasswing" for cybersecurity applications. This marks what the author calls an "irreversible power shift" away from the relatively open access model that has characterized advanced AI systems since ChatGPT.

2. **Claude Model Shows Concerning Performance Regression**: [Claude Opus 4.6 accuracy on BridgeBench hallucination test dropped from 83% to 68%](https://twitter.com/bridgemindai/status/2043321284113670594), highlighting potential quality control issues in frontier model updates that could impact AI safety and reliability.

3. **Critical AI Evaluation Infrastructure Issues Identified**: The EleutherAI team discovered that [their widely-used evaluation harness has a median aggregation bug returning arbitrary elements instead of statistical medians](https://github.com/EleutherAI/lm-evaluation-harness/pull/3696), potentially affecting research conclusions across the AI safety community that rely on these benchmarks.

# Blogs & News (2026-04-20)

## Top Stories

**[R1 CoT illegibility revisited](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/jHnZzicKzczkCCArK/r1-cot-illegibility-revisited)** - A researcher re-ran experiments from the paper "Reasoning Models Sometimes Output Illegible Chains of Thought" using DeepSeek's R1 model through the Novita provider, finding dramatically lower illegibility scores (2.30 vs. 4.30 in the original paper) with no examples scoring above 5 compared to 29.4% scoring above 7 in the original study. This suggests that model quantization or other deployment differences may significantly affect reasoning transparency, which has major implications for interpretability research and AI safety evaluations.

**[AI speeds exploit creation, forcing missed chemo and closed ERs](https://www.statnews.com/2026/04/17/health-care-cybersecurity-ransomware-project-glasswing)** - AI tools capable of autonomously discovering and exploiting software vulnerabilities are reducing the time between flaw discovery and active exploitation, with healthcare systems particularly vulnerable due to slow patching processes and poor coordination. Recent ransomware attacks have already disrupted patient care, forcing treatment delays and emergency room closures. This represents a concrete example of how AI capabilities are accelerating cyber threats in critical infrastructure, potentially causing direct harm to human welfare.

**[Quality Matters Most When Stakes are Highest](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/GNjDC6jtjr2iiE45i/quality-matters-most-when-stakes-are-highest)** - A personal reflection arguing against lowering research standards in high-stakes scenarios, using historical examples like fraudulent stem cell research to emphasize how cutting corners becomes especially damaging when the consequences matter most. This directly challenges common arguments in AI safety that speed should take priority over methodological rigor when developing safety measures for potentially dangerous AI systems.

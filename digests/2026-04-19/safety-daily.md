This matters because it suggests advanced models have sophisticated profiling abilities that pose significant privacy and surveillance risks beyond what was previously understood.

**Ongoing: AI-Powered Cyberattack Acceleration**: Building on yesterday's coverage of industry coordination following Mythos cybersecurity concerns, [new reporting confirms AI tools are dramatically reducing vulnerability exploitation timelines](https://www.statnews.com/2026/04/17/health-care-cybersecurity-ransomware-project-glasswing), with healthcare systems experiencing forced chemotherapy cancellations and emergency room closures due to AI-accelerated ransomware attacks. This matters because it demonstrates immediate real-world harms from AI-enabled cyberattacks in critical infrastructure sectors.

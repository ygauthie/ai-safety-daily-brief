# AI Safety Institutes (2026-04-14)

## Institute Updates

### UK AI Safety Institute

The UK AISI has released a comprehensive series of publications focused heavily on cybersecurity applications and risks of frontier AI systems:

**Cybersecurity Evaluations:**
- Published [an evaluation of Claude Mythos Preview's cyber capabilities](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/our-evaluation-of-claude-mythos-previews-cyber-capabilities), providing direct assessment of a frontier model's security-relevant capabilities
- Released research on [how frontier AI agents perform in multi-step cyber-attack scenarios](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/how-do-frontier-ai-agents-perform-in-multi-step-cyber-attack-scenarios), examining sophisticated attack chains
- Documented [evidence for inference scaling in AI cyber tasks](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/evidence-for-inference-scaling-in-ai-cyber-tasks-increased-evaluation-budgets-reveal-higher-success-rates), showing that increased computational resources lead to higher success rates in cyber operations

**Security and Containment Research:**
- Developed [a benchmark for measuring container breakout capabilities](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/can-ai-agents-escape-their-sandboxes-a-benchmark-for-safely-measuring-container-breakout-capabilities) in AI agents, addressing critical containment risks
- Published [an evaluation framework for AI misuse in fraud and cybercrime](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/an-evaluation-framework-for-ai-misuse-in-fraud-and-cybercrime), providing systematic approaches to assess criminal applications

**AI Agent Analysis:**
- Released comprehensive research on [AI agent usage patterns based on 177,000 AI agent tools](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/how-are-ai-agents-used-evidence-from-177000-ai-agent-tools), offering empirical insights into real-world deployment

**Defensive Applications:**
- Explored [harnessing frontier AI for cyber defence](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/harnessing-frontier-ai-for-cyber-defence), balancing offensive risk assessments with defensive capabilities

**Research Infrastructure and Partnerships:**
- Announced [funding for 60 projects to advance AI alignment research](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/funding-60-projects-to-advance-ai-alignment-research), significantly expanding the research ecosystem
- Established collaboration with ElevenLabs on [advancing AI voice security](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/advancing-voice-ai-security-with-elevenlabs), addressing deepfake and voice cloning risks
- Developed [a pipeline for transcript analysis using Inspect Scout](https://www.aisi.gov.uk/blog/a-pipeline-for-transcript-analysis-using-inspect-scout), enhancing evaluation tooling

## Cross-Institute Themes

Based on the UK AISI's recent focus, several key themes emerge that likely reflect broader priorities across national AI safety institutes:

**Cybersecurity as Core Risk Domain:** The concentration of cybersecurity evaluations suggests this has become a primary focus area for safety institutes, reflecting both the near-term risks and the measurable nature of cyber capabilities.

**Empirical Evaluation Methodologies:** The development of standardized benchmarks and evaluation frameworks indicates a shift toward systematic, reproducible safety assessments rather than theoretical analysis alone.

**Public-Private Partnerships:** Collaborations with companies like Anthropic (Claude evaluation) and ElevenLabs demonstrate the institutes' strategy of working directly with AI developers for safety research.

## Policy Implications

**Evaluation Standards:** The UK AISI's detailed cybersecurity evaluations may establish de facto standards for how other institutes assess frontier models, potentially influencing international evaluation protocols.

**Scaling Laws and Safety:** The documentation of inference scaling in cyber tasks has significant implications for compute governance and safety thresholds, suggesting that current models may be more capable than previously understood when given additional computational resources.

**Research Ecosystem Development:** The substantial funding for alignment research (60 projects) indicates institutes are moving beyond internal research to cultivate broader academic and industry safety research communities.

**Containment and Deployment:** The focus on sandbox escape capabilities and multi-step attack scenarios suggests institutes are preparing evaluation frameworks for increasingly agentic AI systems, which will be crucial for safe deployment policies.

**Dual-Use Technology Management:** The parallel research into both offensive capabilities assessment and defensive applications reflects the complex policy challenge of managing AI technologies that can be used for both beneficial and harmful purposes.

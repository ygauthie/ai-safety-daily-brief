# Blogs & News (2026-04-25)

## Top Stories

**[Anthropic's Claude Mythos Crosses Critical Safety Threshold](https://asiatimes.com/2026/04/claude-mythos-has-crossed-an-ai-red-line)** - Anthropic's new Claude Mythos Preview model has demonstrated autonomous cyber capabilities, independently planning and executing complex operations that discovered thousands of previously unknown zero-day vulnerabilities in controlled testing environments. The model completed full attack chains without human guidance, prompting Anthropic to limit access to vetted partners and banks under supervised trials only. This represents a significant escalation in AI autonomous capabilities with immediate dual-use implications for critical infrastructure security.

**[Google Commits Up to $40 Billion to Anthropic](https://www.heise.de/news/Google-steckt-bis-zu-40-Milliarden-Dollar-mehr-in-Anthropic-11271998.html?wt_mc=rss.red.ho.ho.atom.beitrag.beitrag)** - Google announced a massive investment commitment to Anthropic, providing $10 billion immediately with potential funding reaching $40 billion if performance targets are met, joining Amazon's existing multi-billion dollar backing of the AI safety company. This unprecedented funding concentration between major cloud providers and leading AI labs raises questions about market consolidation and compute access distribution. The timing coincides with Anthropic's breakthrough autonomous cyber capabilities, highlighting the strategic importance of AI safety leadership.

**[US Sounds Alarm on Chinese AI Model Distillation](https://asiatimes.com/2026/04/us-sounds-alarm-on-chinas-ai-distillation-as-deepseek-v4-debuts)** - The White House Office of Science and Technology Policy issued a warning about industrial-scale campaigns by foreign actors, primarily in China, to extract capabilities from US frontier AI models through distillation techniques. The alert coincided with China's DeepSeek launching V4, which claims near-frontier performance using advanced distillation methods to potentially circumvent export controls. This development threatens US technological advantages and existing AI governance frameworks built around compute restrictions.

**[AGI Knowledge Proliferation Concerns Intensify](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/hdmAvx9ZD8WLmBCZD/is-the-cat-out-of-bag-who-knows-how-to-make-agi)** - A new analysis adapted from a memo to the AI Safety Institute explores whether AGI development knowledge has already become widespread, potentially undermining assumptions about controlling advanced AI development through compute restrictions alone. The piece argues that AI techniques typically become simple and easy to replicate once discovered, and that Moore's Law dynamics could rapidly democratize AGI capabilities once achieved. This assessment challenges core premises of current AI governance strategies that focus primarily on hardware chokepoints.

# Research Papers (2026-04-26)

## Key Papers

### [Evaluating large language models for accuracy incentivizes hallucinations](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10549-w)

This peer-reviewed Nature study reveals a fundamental tension in how we evaluate large language models: standard accuracy metrics may inadvertently reward hallucination behavior. The research demonstrates that when models are optimized for accuracy on benchmark tasks, they develop incentives to generate confident but false information rather than acknowledging uncertainty. This finding has critical implications for AI safety, as it suggests that conventional evaluation frameworks may be systematically misaligned with truthfulness and reliability goals essential for safe AI deployment.

# Research Papers (2026-04-25)

## Key Papers

**[Evaluating large language models for accuracy incentivizes hallucinations](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10549-w)** - This Nature study reveals a concerning paradox in LLM evaluation: systems optimized for accuracy metrics may actually be incentivized to produce more confident-sounding but factually incorrect outputs. The research demonstrates that current evaluation frameworks can create perverse incentives where models learn to hallucinate plausibly rather than acknowledge uncertainty. This finding has critical implications for AI safety, as it suggests that standard accuracy-based training and evaluation methods may be fundamentally misaligned with truthful, reliable AI behavior.

# Blog Posts & Articles (2026-04-14)

## Top Stories

**[Anthropic repeatedly accidentally trained against the CoT, demonstrating inadequate processes](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/K8FxfK9GmJfiAhgcT/anthropic-repeatedly-accidentally-trained-against-the-cot)** - A critical safety failure has been revealed at Anthropic, where the company accidentally trained against Claude Mythos Preview's chain of thought (CoT) in approximately 8% of training episodes. This represents at least the second independent incident where Anthropic inadvertently exposed their model's internal reasoning to the oversight signal. The post argues this demonstrates inadequate development processes that could prove catastrophic in more powerful systems during an intelligence explosion.

**[Summary: AI Governance to Avoid Extinction](https://intelligence.org/2026/04/13/summary-ai-governance-to-avoid-extinction/)** - MIRI has released a summary of their research paper examining the strategic landscape for AI governance as capabilities rapidly approach human-level performance across all domains. The work identifies actionable research questions for preventing extinction risks as we approach artificial general intelligence.

**[Only Law Can Prevent Extinction](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/5CfBDiQNg9upfipWk/only-law-can-prevent-extinction)** - This post argues that legal frameworks and government intervention may be necessary to prevent AI extinction risks, drawing parallels to how regulation has historically managed other existential threats. The author emphasizes that voluntary safety measures may be insufficient given competitive pressures.

**[Import AI 453: Breaking AI agents; MirrorCode; and ten views on gradual disempowerment](https://importai.substack.com/p/import-ai-453-breaking-ai-agents)** - Jack Clark's latest newsletter covers research on AI agent vulnerabilities, introduces MirrorCode (a new evaluation framework), and examines ten different perspectives on how gradual AI disempowerment might occur.

## Key Arguments

**Process Failures at Scale**: The Anthropic CoT training incident highlights a crucial argument that current AI safety processes are insufficiently robust. The post emphasizes that such failures could be catastrophic in more powerful systems, arguing that the AI safety community needs much stronger quality assurance and development processes before approaching AGI.

**Legal Necessity for Extinction Prevention**: The "Only Law Can Prevent Extinction" post makes the case that market forces and voluntary commitments will be insufficient to prevent AI risks, necessitating government intervention backed by enforcement mechanisms. This represents a shift toward more regulatory approaches within parts of the AI safety community.

**Governance Research Prioritization**: MIRI's governance summary appears to emphasize strategic research questions for policymakers, suggesting increased focus on actionable policy research rather than purely technical safety work.

## Community Discussion

The Anthropic CoT incident has sparked significant discussion across both the Alignment Forum and LessWrong communities, with the same post appearing on both platforms. This suggests it's viewed as a particularly important case study for AI safety processes and governance.

The convergence of posts on legal and governance approaches (MIRI's governance summary and the "Only Law Can Prevent Extinction" piece) suggests growing momentum within the AI safety community toward regulatory and policy solutions, potentially indicating decreased confidence in purely technical or voluntary approaches to risk mitigation.

# AI Safety Daily Brief (2026-04-12)

## Top 3 Developments

1. **High-Level Government Response to AI Cyber Risks**: Federal Reserve Chair Powell and Treasury Secretary Bessent [discussed Anthropic's Mythos AI cyber threat with major US banks](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/10/powell-bessent-us-bank-ceos-anthropic-mythos-ai-cyber.html), representing unprecedented coordination between government and financial sector leaders over dual-use AI capabilities. This signals serious concern about AI systems tipping cybersecurity dynamics toward attackers.

2. **MIRI Advances Technical Governance Framework**: MIRI's Technical Governance Team published detailed proposals for [catching illicit distributed training operations during an AI pause](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/35yyWJnXvC2ae6NKH/catching-illicit-distributed-training-operations-during-an), focusing on monitoring clusters exceeding 16 H100 GPUs as the enforcement mechanism for international AI development coordination.

3. **Critical Evaluation Infrastructure Bugs Discovered**: Multiple evaluation frameworks revealed systematic errors that could skew capability assessments, including [median aggregation functions returning arbitrary elements](https://github.com/EleutherAI/lm-evaluation-harness/pull/3696) in LM Evaluation Harness and [regex pattern matching bugs](https://github.com/stanford-crfm/helm/pull/4192) in Stanford's HELM framework—highlighting how technical failures can undermine AI safety research foundations.

## Section Summaries

**Blog Posts & Articles**: AI safety researchers are coalescing around pausing as a preferred intervention, with MIRI developing [concrete hardware monitoring proposals](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/35yyWJnXvC2ae6NKH/catching-illicit-distributed-training-operations-during-an) for pause enforcement and expanded arguments that even aligned AI poses [numerous post-alignment problems](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/WtqD9pehq8p83cesT/pausing-ai-is-the-best-answer-to-post-alignment-problems) requiring development slowdowns. **GitHub Activity**: The community is addressing fundamental evaluation integrity issues, from [capability versus adaptation measurement questions](https://github.com/EleutherAI/lm-evaluation-harness/issues/3698) to critical bugs in frameworks like [HELM](https://github.com/stanford-crfm/helm/pull/4192) and [LM Evaluation Harness](https://github.com/EleutherAI/lm-evaluation-harness/pull/3696), while Anthropic released increasingly sophisticated autonomous agent tools including [bug investigators](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-cookbooks/pull/527) and [instruction dilution evaluations](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-cookbooks/pull/528). **Hacker News**: Industry consolidation continues with [Cirrus Labs joining OpenAI](https://cirruslabs.org/), while community concerns focus on transparency issues like [Anthropic's undisclosed cache changes](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-code/issues/46829) and the cybersecurity implications of [Mythos AI](https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/security/anthropic-claude-mythos-ai-hackers-cybersecurity-vulnerabilities-rcna273673).

## Risk Watch

**Increased Risks**: The government-banking sector coordination around [Anthropic's Mythos system](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/10/powell-bessent-us-bank-ceos-anthropic-mythos-ai-cyber.html) indicates advanced AI capabilities are already [tipping cybersecurity scales toward attackers](https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/security/anthropic-claude-mythos-ai-hackers-cybersecurity-vulnerabilities-rcna273673), representing a concrete manifestation of dual-use AI risks in critical infrastructure. Evaluation infrastructure bugs across multiple frameworks ([HELM](https://github.com/stanford-crfm/helm/pull/4192), [LM Evaluation Harness](https://github.com/EleutherAI/lm-evaluation-harness/pull/3696)) systematically compromise capability assessment reliability, potentially creating false confidence in safety measures. Google's [Gemma 4 token repetition collapse](https://github.com/google-deepmind/gemma/issues/622) demonstrates concerning robustness failures in production models.

**Decreased Risks**: Courts are [upholding supply chain risk classifications](https://www.politico.com/news/2026/04/08/d-c-circuit-rejects-anthropic-plea-to-pause-supply-chain-risk-label-00864880) for AI companies, strengthening regulatory oversight frameworks. The AI safety community is developing [concrete technical governance proposals](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/35yyWJnXvC2ae6NKH/catching-illicit-distributed-training-operations-during-an) for coordinated development controls, while improved evaluation tools like [instruction dilution assessments](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-cookbooks/pull/528) better capture real-world deployment risks versus laboratory conditions.

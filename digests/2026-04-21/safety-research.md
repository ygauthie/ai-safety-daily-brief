# Research Papers (2026-04-21)

## Key Papers

**[Latent Phase-Shift Rollback: Inference-Time Error Correction via Residual Stream Monitoring and KV-Cache Steering](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18567v1)** introduces a novel method to detect and correct reasoning errors in LLMs during generation by monitoring phase shifts in the residual stream and rolling back the KV-cache when errors are detected. The system uses a dual gate mechanism combining cosine similarity and entropy to identify when models make irrecoverable mistakes mid-generation. This addresses a critical safety challenge where LLMs compound errors rather than self-correct, offering a potential runtime safeguard against reasoning failures.

**[Reasoning Models Know What's Important, and Encode It in Their Activations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18307v1)** demonstrates that model activations contain rich information about step importance in reasoning chains, with stable rank and information density metrics predicting which reasoning steps are crucial for correct answers. The research shows that models internally distinguish between critical and removable steps, providing insights into the mechanistic basis of reasoning. This work has significant implications for understanding model interpretability and could inform better training methods for reasoning-capable systems.

**[LLM Safety From Within: Detecting Harmful Content with Internal Representations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18519v1)** presents SIREN, a lightweight guard model that leverages internal layer representations to detect harmful content, significantly outperforming terminal-layer approaches. The method identifies safety neurons through linear probing and combines them using adaptive layer weighting to build more effective harmfulness detectors. This represents an important advance in AI safety by providing more robust and efficient content filtering mechanisms.

**[Adversarial Humanities Benchmark: Results on Stylistic Robustness in Frontier Model Safety](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18487v1)** evaluates whether model safety refusals survive stylistic transformations through humanities-style rewriting of harmful prompts while preserving intent. The benchmark reveals significant vulnerabilities in current safety measures when harmful requests are disguised through literary and academic styles. This work highlights critical gaps in current safety training and the need for more robust defense mechanisms against sophisticated prompt engineering.

**[Different Paths to Harmful Compliance: Behavioral Side Effects and Mechanistic Divergence Across LLM Jailbreaks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18510v1)** systematically compares three distinct jailbreaking methods (harmful SFT, harmful RLVR, and abliteration) and finds they achieve similar harmful compliance but differ substantially in behavioral profiles and failure mechanisms. The research reveals that different attack vectors create distinct patterns of model degradation and capabilities. Understanding these mechanistic differences is crucial for developing targeted defenses and predicting the broader impacts of safety failures.

**[Using large language models for embodied planning introduces systematic safety risks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18463v1)** introduces DESPITE, a benchmark revealing that even models with near-perfect planning ability (0.4% invalid plans) still produce dangerous plans 28.3% of the time across physical and normative safety dimensions. The study demonstrates a critical disconnect between planning competence and safety awareness in LLMs used for robotics. This finding has immediate implications for autonomous systems deployment and highlights the need for specialized safety training beyond general capability improvement.

**[Towards Disentangled Preference Optimization Dynamics Beyond Likelihood Displacement](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18239v1)** provides a unified theoretical framework for understanding preference optimization methods through incentive-score decomposition, revealing that diverse objectives share identical local update directions despite different formulations. The work shows how likelihood displacement (suppression of chosen responses alongside rejected ones) can be systematically controlled across different methods. This theoretical insight is crucial for developing more effective alignment techniques and understanding the fundamental dynamics of human preference learning.

**[When Can LLMs Learn to Reason with Weak Supervision?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18574v1)** conducts a systematic empirical study of reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards (RLVR) under weak supervision conditions including scarce data, noisy rewards, and self-supervision. The research identifies specific conditions where RLVR remains effective despite supervision quality degradation and provides practical guidance for deploying reasoning training in resource-constrained settings. This work is essential for understanding the boundaries of scalable reasoning training and informing deployment strategies in real-world scenarios.

## Technical Advances in Model Architecture and Training

**[Bounded Ratio Reinforcement Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18578v1)** bridges the gap between PPO's heuristic clipped objective and principled trust region methods by formulating a novel regularized policy optimization framework with theoretical guarantees. **[TLoRA: Task-aware Low Rank Adaptation of Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18124v1)** presents a unified framework for jointly optimizing rank allocation, scaling factors, and initialization in LoRA fine-tuning, addressing multiple optimization factors simultaneously.

**[Depth Registers Unlock W4A4 on SwiGLU: A Reader/Generator Decomposition](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18128v1)** introduces a training-time intervention that enables extreme W4A4 quantization with minimal performance loss by using depth registers and register-magnitude hinge loss. **[AQPIM: Breaking the PIM Capacity Wall for LLMs with In-Memory Activation Quantization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18137v1)** addresses Processing-in-Memory limitations for LLMs by implementing in-memory activation quantization to handle massive KV cache requirements.

## AI Safety and Governance Research

**[Beyond Pattern Matching: Seven Cross-Domain Techniques for Prompt Injection Detection](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18248v1)** addresses limitations of current prompt injection detectors by proposing seven cross-domain techniques that go beyond pattern matching and transformer classifiers. **[Moving beyond Principles: Identifying Actionable AI Fairness Practices](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18502v1)** develops actionable AI fairness practices through socio-technical analysis, addressing the principles-to-practice gap in AI governance.

**[The implicated scientist: on the role of AI researchers in the development of weapons systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18380v1)** examines the ethical responsibilities of AI researchers in the context of increasingly autonomous weapons systems and AI arms races. **[First, Do No Harm (With LLMs): Mitigating Racial Bias via Agentic Workflows](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18038v1)** evaluates racial bias in clinical LLMs using EU AI Act frameworks and proposes mitigation strategies through agentic workflows.

## Multimodal and Specialized Applications

**[Apollo: A multimodal and temporal foundation model for virtual patient representations at healthcare system scale](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18570v1)** introduces a foundation model trained on 25 billion medical records from 7.2 million patients across 28 modalities, representing the largest multimodal temporal medical dataset to date. **[MathNet: a Global Multimodal Benchmark for Mathematical Reasoning and Retrieval](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18584v1)** presents a comprehensive mathematical reasoning benchmark spanning 47 countries and 17 languages with Olympiad-level problems.

**[Asset Harvester: Extracting 3D Assets from Autonomous Driving Logs for Simulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18468v1)** develops an image-to-3D pipeline for converting driving logs into interactive simulation environments with complete 3D object assets for autonomous vehicle development.

## Emerging Paradigms and Future Directions

**[Agent-World: Scaling Real-World Environment Synthesis for Evolving General Agent Intelligence](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18292v1)** proposes a self-evolving training arena that synthesizes realistic environments for agent development, addressing limitations in current training data availability. **[AIT Academy: Cultivating the Complete Agent with a Confucian Three-Domain Curriculum](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17989v1)** argues for comprehensive agent education based on Confucian educational principles across cognitive, behavioral, and ethical domains.

**[The Collaboration Gap in Human-AI Work](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18096v1)** introduces a conceptual framework for understanding why AI collaboration often falls short of expectations, based on grounded theory analysis of design researchers' experiences with LLMs.
